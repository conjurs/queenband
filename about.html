About

Queen is a legendary British rock band formed in 1970, consisting of Freddie Mercury (vocals), Brian May (guitar), Roger Taylor (drums), and John Deacon (bass). They became famous for their eclectic mix of rock, opera, pop, and theatrical performances.

Queen was formed when Freddie Mercury, who was originally part of a band called "Smile," joined forces with guitarist Brian May and drummer Roger Taylor, who had previously played together in Smile. John Deacon later joined as the bassist, completing the lineup.

Freddie Mercury (5 September 1946 - 24 November 1991) is often considered one of the greatest rock vocalists of all time, known for his incredible vocal range, charismatic stage presence, and theatrical performances. His personal life, particularly his struggle with AIDS, became a key part of the band's legacy. He passed away in 1991 at the age of 45, but his influence on music and culture remains immense.

Brian May (19 July 1947) The band’s guitarist, known for his unique sound and complex solos. He is also a talented songwriter and astrophysicist.

Roger Taylor (July 26, 1949) Queen’s drummer with energetic rhythms and harmonies. He wrote several songs for the band and occasionally took lead vocals.

John Deacon (August 19, 1951) The quiet and steady bassist, known for his songwriting contributions. He retired from music after Freddie's death and stays out of the public eye.

These four members together created one of the most influential rock bands in history.
